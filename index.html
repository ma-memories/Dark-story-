SUBJECT : ALI SAKR

CASE : #0714

STATUS : UNKNOWN

ACCESS : AUTHORIZED PERSONNEL ONLY

PATIENT FILE

Clinic No.14

Tick... Tack... Tick... Tack...

The room smells like old paper and disinfectant. A leather chair. A wooden desk. A psychiatrist quietly watching the man sitting in front of him.

His name... Ali Sakr.

Psychiatric Evaluation

DOCTOR

"Ali Sakr... Even your name feels heavier than your age. People think strength lives inside a name... but I have been reading your file. Every page. Every note. Every scar."

"You covered your body with ink... Tell me... Are these tattoos punishment... or protection?"

"Did you draw walls around yourself... because people kept finding ways to hurt you?"

ALI

"People love endings... Nobody wants to watch the beginning."

"The smile you're looking at... is cheaper than explaining pain."

"I trusted too much. Waited too long. Gave more than I ever received."

"And every single time... I told myself... 'This one is different.'"

"It never was."

"The people who promised 'We're always with you.' were always the first to disappear."

"So... I stopped explaining. I started smiling."

A smile that simply says... 'What else did you expect?'

Tattoo Analysis

The doctor slowly stands up. He walks around Ali... not like a psychiatrist... but like an investigator examining evidence.

His eyes stop... at the left forearm.

The first sentence appears...

"Try for yourself..."

Below it... two realistic eyes. Watching. Silent. Alive.

Under those eyes... another sentence...

"...and for me."

The word me is written in deep red. Unlike everything else.

From that single word... the body of a realistic snake begins. As if the snake itself... was born from that promise.

The Left Forearm

Evidence 01

The doctor gently touches the red word... me then follows the snake with his eyes.

"So... your rebirth... starts from this single word?"

ALI

"Marwa wasn't a miracle... she was simply the first person who made me see myself differently."

"She didn't create a new version of me... she helped me discover that it already existed."

"The word me isn't written in red because it looks beautiful."

"It's written in red because promises are written with blood... not ink."

The doctor remains silent... watching the snake.

Its scales seem almost alive. Its body wraps around the arm without ever looking aggressive.

Almost... protective.

DOCTOR

"Most people think a snake represents evil."

"So why choose one?"

ALI

"Because people only judge what they see."

"They never ask what something means."

"A snake leaves its old skin behind."

"It never turns back to wear it again."

"And that's exactly what happened to me."

"My old self died."

"My new self was born from the pain."

"This tattoo... isn't about death."

"It's about rebirth."

The Right Forearm

The doctor's attention moves to the other arm.

A bold sentence. Simple. Short. Impossible to ignore.

NO RISK NO STORY

Above it... a Roman date.

II • XIV • MMXXII

The doctor slowly smiles.

"So... this wasn't just a tattoo. It was the day everything changed."

The Meaning of Time

DOCTOR

"So... February 14th, 2022."

"To everyone else... it's only a date."

"But to you... it became permanent."

"You carved it into your skin."

"Why?"

ALI

"Because time split into two lives."

"There was a version of me before that day..."

"And another one after it."

"Most people remember dates because of birthdays."

"I remember mine... because that's the day I started becoming someone else."

"It wasn't easy."

"It wasn't perfect."

"But it was real."

NO RISK NO STORY

Life without risk... is only survival.

No memories. No victories. No lessons.

ALI

"If I'm going to fall... I'll stand again."

"If I'm going to lose... I'll learn."

"But hiding from pain... isn't living."

"It's only waiting."

The Last Shields

The doctor's eyes move upward... toward Ali's neck.

Never Look Back

Then... to the right side of his chest.

Don't Trust Damn Humans

The room becomes silent. The ticking clock is louder than ever.

DOCTOR

"These... feel different."

"They don't look like memories."

"They look... like armor."

The Final Shields

ALI

"'Never Look Back'... isn't a motivational quote."

"It's a command."

"I wrote it on my neck... because every time I wanted to move forward... I kept turning around."

"And every single look toward the past... cost me another piece of myself."

"So now... my own body reminds me to keep walking."

DOCTOR

"And the one on your chest?"

"'Don't Trust Damn Humans.'"

"Most people would call it hate."

ALI

"No..."

"It's not hate."

"It's a scar."

"Trust isn't a right."

"It's a privilege."

"And once it's broken... you never see people the same way again."

"I stopped waiting for apologies."

"I stopped waiting for people to save me."

"Everyone is the hero of their own story."

"And sometimes... we're nothing more than background characters in theirs."

"The ones who leave you when you're weak..."

"...don't deserve a seat when you become strong."

The New Birth

DOCTOR

"So... all these tattoos... were simply an escape?"

ALI

"No..."

"Every tattoo..."

"...was a funeral for someone I no longer wanted to be."

"Every sentence was an apology to myself."

"Every black line closed a wound that words could never heal."

"I wasn't born the day I entered this world."

"I was born the day I buried the old version of myself."

"And I called it..."

NEW BIRTH

FINAL EVIDENCE

The doctor says nothing. He slowly closes the patient's file. For a brief moment... the room becomes completely silent. Only the ticking clock remains.

CASE CLOSED
STATUS : REBORN

The Final Statement

"I wasn't born the day I came into this world. I was born the day I buried the old version of myself."

DARK STORY

Ali slowly walks toward the door... He stops. Without turning around... he smiles. Not because he's happy... But because he finally understands that some stories are never meant to be forgotten.

He turns his head... looks directly at you.

"وفي النهاية... كان معكم المريض النفسي... وشكرًا."

— Ali Sakr
